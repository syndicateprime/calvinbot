The Shorter Catechism - Question 9

Question: What is the work of creation?

Answer: The work of creation is God's making all things out of nothing, by the word of his power, in the space of six days, and all very good.

Understanding the Work of Creation

The work of creation refers to the act where the triune God created the universe from nothing using nothing but his divine power. The Westminster Shorter Catechism teaches that this work of creation occurred in the space of six days and that everything God created was very good.

This idea is supported by numerous biblical passages. The Bible states multiple times that God created everything in existence. Here are a few examples:

  • Genesis 1:1 - "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."
  • Psalm 33:6 - "By the word of the LORD the heavens were made, and by the breath of his mouth all their host."
  • Colossians 1:16 - "For by him all things were created, in heaven and on earth, visible and invisible, whether thrones or dominions or rulers or authoritiesâ€”all things were created through him and for him."

The Importance of the Work of Creation

The work of creation is important for Christians to understand because it reminds us of God's infinite power and wisdom. By creating everything out of nothing, God demonstrated that He alone is the true God deserving of our worship and adoration. The work of creation also reminds us of God's love and care for us. He created us and placed us within a world that He declared was very good, giving us an environment that is perfect for our flourishing.

Challenges to the Work of Creation

Several challenges have arisen in contemporary times regarding the work of creation. Some scientists and secularists reject the idea of a Creator and attribute the existence of the universe to natural events, such as evolution. However, the Bible teaches that the work of creation was entirely intentional and deliberate.

Fortunately, there are resources for Christians to use in order to counteract these challenges. For instance, there exist numerous studies and works exploring the evidence for creation which Christians can use to further understand and articulate the importance of the work of creation. These sources include:

  • The work of creation according to account in Genesis 1.
  • The prevalent motif of God as Creator throughout the Bible.
  • Biblical affirmations of a six-day creation.
  • Biblical evidence of a good creation that reflected God's wisdom and love.
  • Scientific evidence supporting the concept of intelligent design.

Conclusion

Understanding the work of creation is important for all Christians because it reminds us of God's infinite power and wisdom, and of His great love for us. This work also serves as a powerful testimony to God's existence and has been the historical belief of Christianity since the beginning of time.
