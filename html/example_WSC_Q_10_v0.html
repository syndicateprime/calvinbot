What are the decrees of God according to the Westminster Shorter Catechism?

The Westminster Shorter Catechism provides a succinct and comprehensive summary of the core doctrines of Christian faith. Let's explore Question 10:


"What are the decrees of God?"

The decrees of God refer to His eternal plan and purpose for all things, which He established before the foundation of the world.

The Bible teaches that God is sovereign over all aspects of creation and history, and that everything happens according to His will (Isaiah 46:10; Ephesians 1:11). God's decrees are not subject to change or revision, and everything that happens is within the scope of His perfect plan (Numbers 23:19).


Understanding God's Decrees

God's decrees are the manifestation of His perfect and infinite wisdom, knowledge, and love. They are designed to bring about the greatest possible glory to Him and to accomplish His purposes in the world.

God's decrees are mysterious and beyond human comprehension, but they are revealed to us in Scripture so that we may understand what He has revealed about Himself and His plans.

The Bible tells us that all of God's decrees are good, righteous, and holy (Romans 8:28; Psalm 119:68). They are designed to promote the welfare of His people and the advancement of His Kingdom on earth.

The Relationship between God's Decrees and Human Responsibility

One of the most challenging aspects of understanding God's decrees is how they relate to human responsibility. The Bible teaches that human beings are responsible for their actions and choices, and that we will be held accountable for them on the Day of Judgment (Romans 14:12).

At the same time, the Bible also teaches that nothing happens outside of God's sovereign will, and that even the sinful actions of human beings are part of His overall plan (Genesis 50:20; Acts 2:23).

While these two ideas may seem contradictory or paradoxical, they can be reconciled when we consider God's infinite wisdom and knowledge. God's decrees do not negate human responsibility; rather, they encompass it and work together with it to accomplish His purposes.

The Implications of God's Decrees for Christians

As Christians, our understanding of God's decrees serves to give us comfort, hope, and assurance. We know that God is in control of all things, and that He is using even the difficult and painful circumstances of life for our ultimate good (Romans 8:28). We can rest in the knowledge that God's plan for us is perfect, and that His grace is sufficient for us in all circumstances (2 Corinthians 12:9).

Conclusion

The Westminster Shorter Catechism teaches that the decrees of God are His eternal plan and purpose for all things. These decrees are designed to bring about the greatest possible glory for Him and to accomplish His purposes in the world. While the relationship between God's decrees and human responsibility may seem paradoxical, they work together to accomplish God's perfect plan. As Christians, we can take comfort in knowing that God is in control of all things, and that His grace is sufficient for us in all circumstances.
