The Chief End of Man According to the Westminster Shorter Catechism

What is the Chief End of Man?

The Westminster Shorter Catechism is a summary of Christian beliefs that was written in the 17th century. It has been used by churches and Christians around the world as a tool for teaching and learning about basic Christian doctrine. The very first question in the catechism asks, "What is the chief end of man?" The answer is, "Man's chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever."

What does this mean? It means that our primary purpose in life is to honor and glorify God with everything we do and say, and to find true satisfaction and fulfillment in our relationship with Him. This is reflected in many passages of the Bible:

  • Psalm 34:1, "I will bless the Lord at all times; his praise shall continually be in my mouth."
  • 1 Corinthians 10:31, "So, whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do all to the glory of God."
  • Revelation 4:11, "Worthy are you, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power, for you created all things, and by your will they existed and were created."

We were created by God to bring Him glory and to find joy and satisfaction in our relationship with Him. It is only when we are in right relationship with God that we can truly experience the fullness of life that He has for us.

"It is the main end of man to glorify God, and to enjoy Him forever." - Westminster Shorter Catechism

What Does it Mean to Glorify God?

To glorify God means to give Him the honor and adoration that He deserves. It means recognizing that God is the Creator of all things, and that everything we have and are comes from Him. It means acknowledging His goodness, His wisdom, and His power. When we glorify God, we are magnifying Him in our hearts and minds, and showing the world around us what an amazing God He is.

One way we can glorify God is by obeying His commandments. Jesus said, "If you love me, you will keep my commandments" (John 14:15). When we follow God's commands, we are showing Him that we love Him and that we trust Him. We are also demonstrating to others what it looks like to live a life that honors God.

Another way we can glorify God is by living a life of gratitude. When we recognize all that God has done for us, we can't help but be thankful. We can show our gratitude to God by thanking Him in prayer, by telling others about His goodness, and by using the gifts and talents He has given us to serve others.

"Do all the good you can, in all the ways you can, to all the souls you can, in every place you can, at all the times you can, with all the zeal you can, as long as ever you can." - John Wesley

What Does it Mean to Enjoy God?

To enjoy God means to find our ultimate satisfaction and fulfillment in Him. It means recognizing that God is the source of all good things, and that apart from Him, we can do nothing. When we enjoy God, we are finding our joy and peace in Him, rather than in the things of this world.

One way we can enjoy God is by spending time with Him in prayer and reading His Word. As we draw near to God, He draws near to us (James 4:8), and we experience the peace and joy that only He can give. Another way we can enjoy God is by being in fellowship with other believers. When we gather together with other Christians, we encourage one another and build each other up (1 Thessalonians 5:11).

Ultimately, enjoying God means that we are finding our rest and our hope in Him. We can trust Him with our lives and our future, knowing that He is good and that He has a plan and purpose for us. When we delight in God, we are able to face the trials of life with grace and courage, knowing that our ultimate victory is in Him.
