Question 6 of The Westminster Shorter Catechism Explained

Question 6 of the Westminster Shorter Catechism is, "What do the Scriptures principally teach?"

Answer:

The Scriptures principally teach what man is to believe concerning God, and what duty God requires of man (2 Timothy 1:13, 2 Timothy 3:16-17)


The answer to this question highlights the two main subject areas of the Holy Scriptures: What man is to believe concerning God, and what duty God requires of man. In other words, the Scriptures are primarily focused on beliefs about God and how we should live according to His will.


Here are a few Bible verses that support this notion:

  • 2 Timothy 1:13 - "Hold on to the pattern of sound teaching that you have heard from me, with the faith and love that are in Christ Jesus."
  • 2 Timothy 3:16-17 - "All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."

Throughout the Bible, we see countless examples of God teaching His people the truth about who He is and how they should live in relationship with Him. From the Ten Commandments in Exodus 20 to the teachings of Jesus in the New Testament, the Scriptures are filled with instruction about how we should think and act as followers of God.


Ultimately, the Bible shows us that God is both loving and just, and that we are called to love and serve Him with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength.
