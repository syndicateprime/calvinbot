How Many Persons are There in the Godhead?

The Westminster Shorter Catechism is a document that provides a concise summary of Christian doctrine. It begins by answering one of the most fundamental questions of the faith: "What is God?" It then goes on to answer an important follow-up question: "How many persons are there in the Godhead?" The answer to this question reveals an essential aspect of Christian belief.

The answer: Three Persons

The Westminster Shorter Catechism’s answer to the question of how many persons are in the Godhead is simply: "There are three persons in the Godhead; the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost; and these three are one God, the same in substance, equal in power and glory."

This statement is an important reflection of what the Bible teaches about God. This belief is called the doctrine of the Trinity. It expresses the idea that there is one God who eternally exists in three distinct persons: the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit. Each person of the Trinity is equally God, and together they make up the one Godhead.

Biblical support for the Trinity

Although the word "Trinity" is not found in the Bible, the doctrine is thoroughly biblical. The Bible teaches that there is one God (Deuteronomy 6:4), and yet it also identifies three distinct persons as God: the Father (1 Peter 1:2), the Son (John 1:1), and the Holy Spirit (Acts 5:3-4).

Here are some Bible verses that support the doctrine of the Trinity:

  • Matthew 28:19 - "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."
  • 2 Corinthians 13:14 - "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all."
  • John 14:16-17 - "And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another Helper, to be with you forever, even the Spirit of truth, whom the world cannot receive, because it neither sees him nor knows him. You know him, for he dwells with you and will be in you."
  • 1 Peter 1:2 - "Elect according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, through sanctification of the Spirit, unto obedience and sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ."
  • Ephesians 4:4-6 - "There is one body and one Spirit—just as you were called to the one hope that belongs to your call—one Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and Father of all, who is over all and through all and in all."
  • Isaiah 48:16 - "And now the Lord God has sent me, and his Spirit."

The importance of the doctrine of the Trinity

The doctrine of the Trinity is not just an abstract theological concept; it has practical implications for the daily life of believers. Understanding the three persons of the Trinity helps us understand God's character and the different ways in which He relates to us.

For example, the Father is the creator of all, the Son is our Savior who died on the cross for us, and the Holy Spirit empowers us to live the life God has called us to. This understanding of the Trinity can help us relate to each person of the Godhead in a unique way and cultivate a deeper, more meaningful relationship with God as a whole.
